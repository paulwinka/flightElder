About Me

Thanks for visiting my website. I'm Tink Sullivan. Yes, Tinkler is my first name. :) I've heard all the jokes before...

My site name "flightelder" explains a lot about who I am and what is most important to me in life. I learned to fly helicopters in the Air Force and still work as a helicopter pilot today. Also, I am an elder and advisor at my church. I am also getting close to 60 years old, so I wear the label "elder" all too well!

You may have noticed the video playing in the background when opening this page. What is going on there, where is it? This is Gateway City Church just before Sunday Service starts at 1000--it's my favorite moment of the week. Here is the church's location. The positive vibe from the presence of God in the room is addictive and quenches the soul. I am incredibly proud to be a elder of this church.

I now work hard at balancing all aspects of life. I will never say I have it all figured out, but I have learned a few things as I have matured. Before I came to know God I focused my attention at chasing money and debauchery while neglecting my health, spirituality, and family. It left me feeling empty inside, like there had to be something more. That's what brought me to God. For me, it's a better way to live life than I was doing before I was baptized, it's like choosing between a bed of nails of a bed of feathers. I'll how I am living now each time.

Also, please feel free to get in touch or leave a comment on one of the pages.

My job as a helicopter pilot.

I work for Air Evac Lifeteam, an air medical emergency service provider located in O’Fallon, Missouri, There I operate a state-of-the-art helicopter flight simulator. The $3 million simulator — manufactured by Frasca International — is owned by Air Evac Lifeteam,. The U.S. Navy, German police and Emirate Airlines have all sent representatives to test the helicopter flight simulator.

The simulator uses pneumatic servos to simulate the motion of flying and landing. Laser projections can instantly change the simulated landscape and weather conditions. The flight training device also prepares pilots for a variety of emergency maneuvers.

Air Evac Lifeteam has 130 medical bases in 15 states. More than 500 pilots work for the company and all will spend time on the simulator to improve flying skills.

    • Gateway City Church in St, Louis, MO

    Gateway City Church began in 1986 as the Gateway Church of Christ with a dream of being and building Jesus’ church according to the Scriptures.

    Since that time, we have grown from a handful of Christians to an attendance of more than 400 every Sunday. We have sent out many of our members to start or strengthen sister congregations throughout the United States and abroad.

    Our congregation is quite diverse in age, race, and socio-economic background, yet is unified by the Spirit of God. We are committed to serving God, one another, and the world around us.

    We are in fellowship with the International Churches of Christ International Churches of Christ . We support benevolent works around the world through HOPEworldwide and missionary churches throughout Eurasia.
